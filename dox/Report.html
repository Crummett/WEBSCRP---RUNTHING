Report

As can be seen above, I was hit by the typical student procrastination. Tasks were dragged out considerably longer than they ever should have taken.

A not insignificant problem was marrying up what we were learning at the given moment, with what I was attempting to do at the time. By the time I was trying to get into using php on my website, we were only just learning the basics, and were yet to touch on pdo. Similarly I failed to understand the api and its importance until quite late on, leading it to become fairly rushed. I found it very difficult to get to grips with the many different layers to web development and its lack of rigid structure. I found myself losing track of where bits of data were coming from and going to. During the course of the year I did start to get to grips with the structure, but transitioning to Node finally sent me down the right path, and development began in earnest.

When we learnt about Node.js in lectures, I was taken aback by how simple it was compared to using the many layers required for server-side php. Eventually I took the decision to re-do everything I had so far in Node, and continue from there. Naturally this caused a hold-up as I had to re-work all of my HTML as ejs templates, and convert my php into client-side JavaScript, however development was considerably faster once I had done this. The JavaScript element of web design has been what I’ve found easiest. It ties in more naturally with the other programming experience I have from other units. The data manipulation was tricky, but I was able to fall back on phpMyAdmin in XAMPP to generate my queries. The visual elements (HTML and CSS) were also stumbling blocks. Trying to articulate what I’m after in terms of layout and design has always felt hard within the confines of programming languages, and a lot of time was spent attempting to get things to line up just right, or have exactly the right coloured borders.

Getting to grips with Node was also aided by the wealth of online resources for JavaScript. When dealing with php, I had struggled to find online answers for many of my questions, but with node I always managed to find a tutorial or stackoverflow question that could nudge me in the correct direction.

The order with which I approached things also changed dramatically. The event pages became my initial focus, essentially as a proof of concept as I worked out what was going on. Once I had transitioned to Node this was again my approach, to get events, with all of the get and post that entails, before applying what I’d learned from that to creating the club and user pages.

Ultimately my struggles with this unit came from a lack of structure all round. Using Node eased the burden, but this was a unit I undoubtedly found very hard. The scale of the coursework felt very daunting - coming up with the concept alone took a lot of thought - and this caused a fair amount of anxiety about where I was going. Hopefully I’ll learn from this.
